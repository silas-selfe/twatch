Findings

  • This is an evening-walk street. Pedestrian traffic climbs all day to a single peak at 7–9 PM — there is no real morning rush on foot. Weekday evenings nearly match weekend evenings; the whole weekend surplus comes from afternoons.
  • Weekend afternoons are 2–3× weekday afternoons. From 1 to 6 PM, weekends average 10–13 pedestrians/hour against 3–7 on weekdays — leisure foot traffic, not commuting.
  • Foot traffic barely registers a commute. Weekday 7–9 AM adds only a small bump (~4–6/hour). Whoever commutes on this street drives.
  • About 1 in 15 is moving at running pace (≥150 px/s; walking clusters at 40–95). Runners skew to mornings and early evenings.
  • The street empties on foot after 10 PM — five pedestrians total across five nights. Vehicles keep flowing until midnight, so evening walkers vanish two hours before drivers do.
  • Relative to cars, walkers own the late evening. The pedestrian share of all traffic doubles from ~1% at midday to ~2.3% around 9 PM, when vehicle volume fades faster than foot traffic.
  • ⚠ caveatNight counts are a lower bound. Pedestrians are small, unlit objects — the detector's night recall is worse for them than for headlit vehicles, so the 10 PM cliff is partly real, partly visibility. Counts are also modest overall (522), so single-hour differences of a few people are noise; the day-type patterns above rest on pooled hours.
