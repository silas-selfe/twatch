Findings

  • This road runs ~9,000–12,000 vehicles a day, and weekdays beat the weekend: Tuesday’s 11,906 is the biggest day recorded, 21% above Saturday. Every day shows a long daytime plateau (600–990 vehicles/hour from noon to 9 PM) rather than sharp twin rush peaks, then falls off a cliff after 10 PM, bottoming at ~20/hour between 3 and 5 AM.
  • Weekday mornings show a commute signature the weekend lacks. At 6 AM, Monday saw 553 vehicles and Tuesday 530 — 2.8× the weekend average for that hour (~190) — roughly 60% headed left→right both days. Weekends instead ramp up gradually from 8 AM to a midday plateau.
  • The 4–6 PM window is the reliable daily maximum: Monday’s 989 and 983 vehicle-hours are the two biggest of the whole period, with Tuesday’s 932 close behind — every day peaks in the same late-afternoon band.
  • Congestion shows up in speed, not just volume. Median car speed overnight is 103–127 px/s but only 57–68 px/s through the day — daytime traffic moves at roughly half the free-flow speed, slowest (57 px/s) at 6–7 PM, exactly the volume peak.
  • Daytime flow skews left→right, ~55/45, most strongly in morning hours; late evening (9–10 PM) tilts back right→left. Rename the direction labels in config.yaml to real bearings to turn this into a proper inbound/outbound story.
  • ⚠ verifyOvernight direction data looks like a detection artifact, not reality. Between 10 PM and 5 AM, 93–99% of counted vehicles read right→left — every night, including weekends; Tuesday’s small hours logged literally zero left→right vehicles. One travel direction is probably nearly invisible to the detector at night (headlight glare vs. taillights). Check a few overnight event snapshots before trusting any night-time direction split.
  • ⚠ gapThe collector was down Monday 8 AM–noon (plus two partial hours around restarts Friday night). Those hours are flagged in every chart and excluded from per-hour averages; Monday's daily total is an undercount.
